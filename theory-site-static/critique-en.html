Self-critique and open questions

For the theory not to become a belief in itself, it must leave room for questions that threaten it.

Strong objections to keep open

This section is not meant to defend the theory at any cost. It is meant to show that the model can stand near difficult questions without turning into false certainty.

The scientific reader

The concern: terms such as P versus NP, black holes or energy may sound as if science is being used to prove metaphysics. The correction: each such term should be marked as metaphor, structural language or formal claim; every formal claim needs a source.

The philosophical reader

The concern: the model may be so wide that it can absorb anything. The correction: keep short, stable definitions of potential, ideal and optimal, and allow expansion only after the base is clear.

The religious reader

The concern: the language touches God, redemption and ideal without locating itself in relation to existing traditions. The correction: clarify that the model is not a new religion, not a theological ruling, and not a replacement for tradition.

The literary reader

The concern: explanation may suffocate the stories. The correction: let the story work where it is strong, and add only a short framing note that places it inside the model.

This is an open thought experiment, not a closed doctrine.
